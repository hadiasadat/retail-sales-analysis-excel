Retail Sales Analysis: From Data to Business Insights
Overview

This project transforms raw retail sales data into actionable business insights using Excel and Python.

The goal is not just to analyze data, but to answer key business questions:
👉 Where is the company making money?
👉 Where is it losing money?
👉 What is driving profit and loss?

🎯 Business Problem

Retail companies often increase discounts to boost sales, but this can negatively impact profitability.

This project investigates:

Are discounts helping or hurting the business?
Which products and regions are truly profitable?
What patterns exist in customer behavior and sales trends?
🔍 Approach

The analysis follows a structured data workflow:

Data Cleaning
Removed duplicates and inconsistencies
Standardized formats (dates, currency, percentages)
Exploratory Analysis
Sales and profit by state, category, and product
Customer and product performance
Time-based trends
Visualization
Built an Excel dashboard to communicate insights clearly
📊 Key Insights
🔥 High discounts (>30%) are strongly linked to negative profit
💻 Technology category generates the highest profit
📉 Some states generate losses despite high sales
📈 Sales peak during Q4 (holiday season)
👥 A small group of customers drives a large portion of revenue
📈 Dashboard

An interactive dashboard was created to present:

Sales trends over time
Profit by category
Discount impact on profitability
Regional performance
🛠️ Tools Used
Excel (Pivot Tables, Dashboard, Data Cleaning)
Python (pandas, matplotlib)

🚀 Conclusion

This project demonstrates how data analysis can support better business decisions by identifying hidden risks (like excessive discounting) and uncovering opportunities for growth.
